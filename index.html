Week 2

IMF credit outstanding

The first chart shows the nominal value of credit outstanding for all IMF members

In real terms

To understand the ability to pay off debts, we must compare debt to income. In this chart we normalise the credit outstanding by dividing through by the value of world GDP each year. (GDP values given in 2015 USD). The results show that although IMF credit payments have increased over the given period, the ability to pay off this debt has increased due to growth in gross income. The most 'risky' year would actually be 1984, where lending was the greatest relative to GDP
